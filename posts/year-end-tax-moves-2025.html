5 Smart Year-End Tax Moves for 2025

November 21, 2025 Taxes Year-End Planning

As 2025 winds down, there's still time to reduce your tax bill - if you make a few smart moves before year-end. Many strategies take effect only if completed by December 31, so acting early gives more flexibility

1) Max Out Retirement Contributions

Contributing to a 401(k) or traditional IRA can lower your taxable income immediately.

  • 401(k) limit: $23,000 for 2025 (+$7,500 catch-up if 50+)
  • IRA limit: $7,000 (+$1,000 catch-up if 50+)
Example: In the 24% bracket, adding $5,000 to your 401(k) before Dec 31 reduces your federal tax by about $1,200 ($5,000 x 24%).

2) Harvest Investment Losses

Sell investments that are down to offset gains (tax-loss harvesting). You can also use up to $3,000 of net capital losses to offset ordinary income each year. Avoid wash sales by waiting 31 days before buying back the same security.

3) Review Withholding & Estimated Taxes

If you had bonuses, freelance income, or big investment gains, check whether you've paid enough tax. Adjust your final 2025 estimated payment (due Jan 15, 2026) to avoid underpayment penalties

4) Consider Charitable Giving Strategies

  • Donate appreciated stock to avoid capital gains and get a deduction if you itemize.
  • Use a donor-advised fund for larger gifts and timing flexibility
  • "Bunch" of multiple years of donations into 2025 if you're near the itemizing threshold.
